eclipt.is, a service by onekilopars.ec    Follow @onekiloparsec

The goal is to provide modern web access to astronomical information, that is, browsable APIs where each resource has its own (that is, unique) Uniform Resource Locator, that is, an URL. Astronomy has often been in the forefront of organising its big data on the web, making available tons of useful information. However, a lack of contact with the web culture makes this information rarely accessible otherwise than through web pages, i.e. readable mostly by humans.

eclipt.is has the ambition to bring some of the web culture to that information, and provide RESTful APIs to different sources of astronomical information, thus enabling to build new apps based on it. As a former professional astronomer, I've written tons of (Python) scripts. Scripts are good, but tend to generate more scripts. That's one of the reason I've built iObserve (also for iPad). An app illustrates the value of integration.
Logically, eclipt.is is also built for being the backend of the coming iObserve 2.

The scope of eclipt.is is potentially enormous. This project is meant to evolve in the coming years.

June 2015.
