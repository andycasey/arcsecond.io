Version 1 (alpha)

Welcome to arcsecond.io

Unified REST APIs for astronomical information

Learn how to feed your next astro app
arcsecond.io aims at providing browsable APIs where each piece of astronomical information has its own Uniform Resource Locator (a.k.a. URL). SIMBAD, NED, exoplanet.eu, JPL Horizons, astro telegrams are among the sources of information that arcsecond.io intend to unify. Because we believe in the force of integration.
arcsecond.io is a service of onekilopars.ec, home of iObserve, the best OS X and iOS apps for astronomers, as well as multiple open-source and FITS-related goodies.

One access point: api.arcsecond.io

Our goal is to give access to as much information as possible, aggregating information with a philosophy centered around resources. The APIs will incrementally be introduced here. To follow what's going on, read onekilopars.ec blog.

Objects

Objects are anything SIMBAD and NED services understand. Enter the name of a known object and press Go! Still incomplete, but more details will be added.

/{{ api_version }}/objects/

Exoplanets

Exoplanets are retrieved from exoplanet.eu. The current implementation is only partially complete, and the access to the whole catalogue will be added.

/{{ api_version }}/exoplanets/

Asteroids & Comets

Asteroids and Comets will be retrieved from the JPL Horizon service.

Observatories & Organisations

Observatories and Organisations are managed directly in arcsecond.io. It will be open for changes to registered users.

Telescopes & Instruments

Telescopes & Instruments are managed directly in arcsecond.io. It will be open for changes to registered users.

Telegrams

Telegrams is high in the whishlist. GRBs, supernovae etc...

Data Archives

Data Archives from ESO, MAST etc. Wouldn't be cool, no? For the moment, I only have ESO programmes summary. But other archives as well as FITS data is planned.

/{{ api_version }}/archives/  /
/summary/

What about astropy?

astropy.org is amazing! If arcsecond.io is the food, astropy is the brain.
In fact, some of the things behind arcsecond.io relies on astropy. Even better, we intend to be as much astropy-compliant as possible.
