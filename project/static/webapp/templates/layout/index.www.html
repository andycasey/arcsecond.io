arcsecond.io aims at unifying the world-wide astronomy data. Our goal is to build a unique place where everything is integrated. And by everything, we mean information about objects, exoplanets, observing sites, solar-system objects, telescopes, people, publications, telegrams, instruments, detectors, finding charts, and access to archived data. And by everything, we also mean your data: observing runs, night logs, reduced data, etc. Because we believe in the force of integration.
arcsecond.io is a service of onekilopars.ec (blog), home of iObserve, the best OS X and iOS apps for astronomers, as well as multiple open-source projects for FITS files, astronomical algorithms, VOTables, etc.

One data access point: api.arcsecond.io

arcsecond.io is being built with modern web and data tools. That is, our services are provided atop RESTful Application Programming Interfaces (APIs). Our goal is to give access to as much information as possible, aggregating information with a philosophy centered around resources.
Visit api.arcsecond.io to explore the details of the APIs.
